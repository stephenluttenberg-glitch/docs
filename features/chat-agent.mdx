<Tip>
Chat is the fastest way to turn OpenSeller into a working business partner instead of a static dashboard.
</Tip>

OpenSeller includes a dedicated **Chat** area in the dashboard, and the same assistant can also live in your messaging channel. The goal isn't just conversation — it's better decisions, faster.

## What Chat is for

Use chat when you want:

- A fast answer about your shop
- A product recommendation
- A listing rewrite
- A strategic second opinion
- A guided next step into another tool

OpenSeller is especially useful when the question touches trends, pricing, listings, shop performance, or workflow priorities.

## Great prompts to start with

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Trend discovery" icon="magnifying-glass-chart">
    `What's trending right now that I could sell?`
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pricing help" icon="tag">
    `Help me price this product at a 40% margin`
  </Card>
  <Card title="Listing optimization" icon="pen-to-square">
    `Find my worst listing and optimize it`
  </Card>
  <Card title="Weekly review" icon="calendar-check">
    `Give me a weekly review of my shop`
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

These work better than broad prompts like "help me grow" because the agent can immediately anchor on a real task.

## Chat + the rest of the app

Chat works best when connected to the rest of your workflow:

1. Ask a question in **Chat**
2. Open a guided workflow in **Use Cases**
3. Review supporting data in **Reports** or **Profit Tracker**
4. Go back to Chat for refinement

That back-and-forth is the intended usage pattern.

## When to use Chat vs a dedicated tool

| Use **Chat** when… | Use a **dedicated tool** when… |
|---|---|
| You're exploring | You need structured inputs |
| You need quick direction | You need charts or tabular outputs |
| You want a rewrite or recommendation | You want repeatable analysis |
| You're not sure which tool to open yet | You're uploading data (like CSVs) |

For example, use Chat to ask about margins, but use **Profit Tracker** when you want actual uploaded order data and true-profit reporting.

## Prompting tips

<Steps>
  <Step title="Give the agent a goal">
    Tell it what you're trying to accomplish, not just what you're curious about.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Include context">
    Mention the marketplace, product type, or price range when relevant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for a specific output">
    "Give me three pricing options" is better than "help with pricing."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow up instead of restarting">
    Continue the thread and refine the request. Continuity gives the agent more context.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Example prompts

- `Optimize this listing for Etsy SEO and explain the tradeoffs`
- `Give me three pricing options and tell me which one protects margin best`
- `Find trend ideas that fit laser engraving and Mother's Day`

## If the answer is too generic

<Note>
Generic inputs create generic outputs. Specific context creates usable outputs.
</Note>

Add one of these to sharpen results:

- Your niche
- Your price range
- Your goal margin
- Your product type
- The exact outcome you want
