<Tip>
The onboarding flow turns OpenSeller from a generic assistant into **your** assistant. You can resume where you left off if you leave mid-flow.
</Tip>

OpenSeller's onboarding is a six-step setup that configures your communication channel, active skills, and shop context.

## The six steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Welcome">
    The first screen explains what OpenSeller is built to do — research trends, track profit, write better listings, and coach you as you grow. Use this step as orientation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your channel">
    Set where your agent lives:

    - **Telegram** — the main mobile-first option
    - **Discord** — also available as a channel option

    Choose the channel you'll actually use during the week. This matters more than picking the "best" option on paper.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your bot">
    After selecting a channel, OpenSeller asks for a bot token and verifies it:

    1. Paste the token
    2. Verify the connection
    3. Confirm the bot username once validation succeeds

    If verification fails, fix the token before moving forward. The rest of the product is still useful, but channel setup is what makes the agent feel immediate.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable skills">
    The onboarding flow includes a skill selection step. Some skills are enabled by default because they're central to the product:

    - **Profit Tracker** — true profit reporting
    - **Listing Writer** — listing optimization
    - **Shop Coach** — personalized coaching
    - **Trend Scout** — trend and niche discovery
    - **Keyword Research** — SEO keyword tools

    Turn on the capabilities that match your current phase. If you're early, trend research and listing help usually matter more than broad automation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Build your shop profile">
    This is where OpenSeller starts learning your business. You can provide:

    - Whether you already have a shop
    - Your shop name
    - What you sell
    - Experience level
    - Revenue range
    - Goals (increasing sales, improving SEO, finding trending products)

    This information directly affects the quality of the agent's recommendations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Launch">
    The final step provisions your setup and sends you into the app. If you've already completed onboarding, OpenSeller skips the flow and sends you straight to the main product.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing setup later

You don't need to redo onboarding to make changes. Use **Settings** to manage:

- Channels
- Skills
- Shop profile
- Notifications
- Plan and model preferences

## Best practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pick the right channel" icon="mobile">
    Choose the one you'll actually respond to, not the one that sounds coolest.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep goals specific" icon="bullseye">
    "Increase sales 20% this quarter" beats "grow my shop."
  </Card>
  <Card title="Don't enable everything" icon="toggle-off">
    Start with 2-3 skills. Add more after a week when you know what you need.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Revisit settings weekly" icon="calendar">
    Come back after a week and tighten your configuration based on real usage.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## If something feels off

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Agent recommendations feel generic">
    Your shop profile is probably too thin. Go to **Settings** and add more detail about what you sell, your price range, and your goals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Responses don't match my niche">
    Your goals may be too broad. Try being specific: "I sell laser-engraved gifts in the $20-40 range" is much better than "I sell stuff on Etsy."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The agent isn't helping with the right things">
    Check that you've connected the right workflow. If you want profit insights but only have Chat enabled, the agent doesn't have the data it needs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Questions get vague answers">
    Ask more specific questions. Include your niche, price range, goal margin, or the exact outcome you want.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
