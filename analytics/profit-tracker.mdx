The Profit Tracker is built for Etsy sellers who need to see real profit after fees, shipping, materials, labor, ads, and refunds. It combines guided setup, uploads, charts, and actionable analysis.

## What Profit Tracker answers

- Which products are actually making money?
- What's my true margin after every cost?
- How much am I losing to fees or shipping?
- What does my monthly P&L really look like?
- What price do I need to hit my target margin?

## Setup flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Profit Tracker">
    Start from **Use Cases** or go directly to **Profit Tracker** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your Etsy data">
    Upload your Etsy statements and listings CSVs so OpenSeller can calculate beyond surface revenue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add cost components">
    Profit Tracker is most valuable when you include the real costs you usually forget:

    - Materials
    - Labor
    - Packaging
    - Shipping
    - Ads
    - Refunds
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create or assign templates">
    Templates let you apply cost assumptions across similar products or variants instead of repeating setup manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your dashboard">
    Focus on the metrics that matter:

    - Total revenue
    - Total true profit
    - Margin percentage
    - Fees and shipping drag
    - Best and worst products
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why "true profit" matters

Revenue alone hides a lot. Profit Tracker surfaces the costs most sellers overlook:

| Hidden cost | Impact |
|---|---|
| Etsy fees | 6.5% transaction + 3-4% payment processing |
| Listing fees | $0.20 per listing, adds up fast |
| Shipping costs | Often underestimated or absorbed |
| Ads | Etsy Ads can eat margins quietly |
| Refunds | Revenue clawbacks you might not track |
| Labor | Your time has a cost too |

## How to use the results

Use Profit Tracker outputs to make real decisions:

- **Raise prices** on low-margin products
- **Cut products** that look good on revenue but fail on margin
- **Reduce shipping drag** by optimizing packaging or rates
- **Identify winning categories** that deserve more traffic
- **Build better reviews** with weekly or monthly P&L check-ins

## Best practices

<Note>
Set up your cost templates **before** judging the dashboard numbers. Partial data leads to misleading conclusions.
</Note>

1. Complete your cost templates before making strategic decisions
2. Don't rely on partial uploads — get the full picture first
3. Use Profit Tracker alongside **Chat** so you can ask follow-up questions about what the numbers mean

<Warning>
**Common mistake:** Looking at revenue first and profit second. The better sequence is the opposite — trust profit first, then decide how revenue growth actually helps the business.
</Warning>
